Project 2

Schvaan

  • Schvaan is a dynamic quadruped composed of 3D-printed parts (80%) and standard hardware (20%). It is a low-cost robot with 12 DOFs, each driven by a proprioceptive actuator. It is capable of executing a leg-homing routine, stand-up routine, and walking with a trot gait. Schvaan is an independent project, completed over a year in a team of 2.



    • Mechanical

    Proprioceptive actuator

  • 1:10 cycloidal gearbox
  • Generic 8308 BLDC at input
  • FOC based driver
    1. Magnetic encoder for position sensing/commutation
    2. Phase current sensing for torque estimation.




  • 3DOFs:

    1. Adduction-Abduction at hip. (about axis||x)
    2. Flexion-extension at shoulder. (about axis||y)
    3. Flexion-extension at knee. (about axis||y)
  • Toothed belt actuator-knee transmission
  • Silicone sock on the foot for improved traction



    • 3-DOF Leg: Problems & Solutions

  • Shorter and single piece tibia (highlighted in green) of the lower leg. The newer tibia was designed to be compatible with the existing knee and foot joints.
  • Knee actuator was moved outwards and flipped 180Â°, thus being better positioned for passive air-cooling of the motor driver.
  • Length of moment arm acting on the hip (adduction-abduction) actuators was reduced.


    • Leg : Problems and Solutions

  • Even though we observed a reliable walking gait in simulations, the actual gait of the robot was quite unstable in the initial iterations.
  • This is because the control was designed assuming a perfectly rigid system, while the robot was comparatively flexible due to 3d-printed parts. The above changes contributed to a significant increase in the rigidity of the robot, and also made the robot more compact. This closed the gap between assumed and actual system, improving the stability of the robot.
