CNC Machine

CNC Milling

This CNC routing machine was the first electro-mechanical project I developed all by myself. I used to do a lot of freelance CAD and manufacturing drawings for companies at the time and felt it would be useful have some way to make physical prototypes in- house. In retrospect, it might have been easier to just buy a small 3d-printer, but those were difficult to come by and even riskier to operate back in 2019. Building this CNC, though, really helped me develop comfort of working on electro-mechanical systems.

The CNC has a workspace of 500mm X 300mm X 80mm, with a frame built out of V-slot aluminum extrusions of various sizes. The table itself it a MDF panel and has a grid of holes with threaded inserts for mounting work. The motion is provided by a toothed belt- pulley system driven by NEMA 23 stepper motors.

I anticipated that the Z-axis would need more rigitdity, so I chose to go with a lead-screw motion system for that. Additionally, using a lead screw allowed finer resolution on the Z axis. This was all controlled by GRBL firmware, running off an Arduino UNO fitted with a CNC shield. I mostly used the router for cutting MDF, polyethylene and acrylic parts. The spindle mount went through various design iterations after I bought my own Ender 3 a few years later. My most difficult projects on this were a PCB fixture with a rather complex contour and milling a cast aluminum plate for the bed of my Voron 2.4 and Trident (this pushed my machine to the limits).
